Sample Article with Mixed Content

The Earth orbits around the Sun in approximately 365.25 days, which is why we have leap years every four years. This is a well-established scientific fact that has been known for centuries.

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues of our time. According to NASA, the planet's average surface temperature has risen about 2 degrees Fahrenheit (1 degrees Celsius) since the late 19th century, largely driven by increased carbon dioxide and other human-made emissions into the atmosphere.

Vaccines have been one of the most successful public health interventions in history. Smallpox, which once killed millions of people annually, was eradicated through a global vaccination campaign. The COVID-19 vaccines developed in 2020-2021 underwent rigorous testing and monitoring for safety and efficacy.

The Great Wall of China is visible from space with the naked eye. This is actually a common misconception - astronauts have reported that while you can see many human-made structures from low Earth orbit with the right conditions, the Great Wall is difficult to distinguish from the surrounding terrain.

Water always drains clockwise in the Northern Hemisphere and counterclockwise in the Southern Hemisphere due to the Coriolis effect. This is actually false - the Coriolis effect is far too weak to affect the direction water drains in a sink or toilet. The direction is determined by the shape of the basin and the initial water movement.
